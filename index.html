About Us

Immigration & Refugee Law
Made Clear.

Mithoowani Waldman Immigration Law Group is a team of lawyers and consultants working in association in Toronto, providing services in all areas of Canadian immigration and refugee law.

We believe in making the law easy to understand, working with you on every step of your matter and fighting for fairness on your behalf.

We are women-led and LGBTQ+ friendly. The immigration system can be intimidating. We will consider every part of your immigration issue and provide you with thorough legal advice — in plain language.

Plain Language Advice

Landmark Supreme Court challenge to the Safe Third Country Agreement. Naseem Mithoowani argued on behalf of interveners that returning claimants to the US violated Charter s. 7 rights to liberty and security.

Supreme Court 2023

Mason v. Canada (Citizenship and Immigration)

Supreme Court clarification of the Vavilov standard of review for immigration decisions, including the interpretation of security inadmissibility under IRPA s. 34(1)(e). Naseem appeared for interveners.

2023 SCC 21 Read Decision ↗
Supreme Court 2015

B010 v. Canada (Citizenship and Immigration)

Supreme Court ruling on people smuggling under IRPA s. 37(1)(b). Successfully argued that a profit motive is required — protecting refugees who helped others flee persecution from being deemed inadmissible.

FAQ

Common Questions

Where is your office located? +

Our office is located at 500 Eglinton Avenue East, Suite 101 in Toronto, Ontario. We ask that you book an appointment in advance before visiting in person. We work both remotely and in our office and will see clients in person only when an appointment has been scheduled.

Can I book a consultation if I'm overseas? +

Yes. We regularly conduct consultations with international clients by phone or video call. Use the booking form above and select "Virtual (Video Call)" or "Phone" as your preferred mode.

Why do I need to book a consultation first? +

A consultation allows us to meet with you and fully understand your legal problem. Consultations run approximately 30 minutes to an hour, depending on complexity, and conclude with a proposed plan of action. If both client and lawyer agree to continue, the lawyer will prepare a retainer agreement.

When should I expect a response? +

We attempt to respond to all inquiries within 24–48 hours. If you have an urgent matter, please call us directly at (416) 792-6077.

Do you represent low income clients? +

Yes. We provide representation to low income and Legal Aid Ontario (LAO) clients on a case by case basis. Please mention this when booking your consultation so we can best assist you.
